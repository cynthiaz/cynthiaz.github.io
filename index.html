ABOUT ME

My design journey started as an industrial design student at Carleton University, where I explored different aspects of design, including research, manufacturing processes, product ergonomics and modelling. I also followed a secondary interest and pursued a minor program in Psychology. During my school years, I interned at companies ranged from product design to graphic design to 3D modelling.

My design spirit is kept alive by the desire to become an intimate part of how things interact with human communities. After I graduated and joined IBM, I transitioned to working on UI design for enterprise software solution. Whether I'm sketching, wireframing or prototyping UIs, I always give a fervent attention to details and strive for best practices to bring intuitive and delightful user experiences to people. Along the way, I have developed a strong passion in user experience design and usability study which combines with my love of psychology. This has focused my vision in discovering user stories, functional aspect of product and how they interact. Aside from design, I also do bit of coding and keep learning new development techniques to help me understand how designs come to life and how they can be improved.

I think design isn't just about ideas or the moment of eureka. It's a process of creative problem solving: to observe, to think, to practice; learning the why and learning to foresee. Now is an exciting time to be in this industry and I'm glad to be a part of it.
