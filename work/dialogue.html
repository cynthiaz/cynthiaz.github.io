Friendly Seats

A versatile seating unit that can be used to facilitate individual and collaborative team work in temporary work areas while encouraging social interaction. It also offers solutions for public seating areas such as cafeterias, schools, libraries, and where work collaboration is in high demand.

The chair has a pivoting backrest that allows the user to flip and adjust it with a locking mechanism to turn it into a small table. The foldable backrest provides two surfaces, padded surface for resting and a writing surface.The table top can accommodate a laptop and larger texts. The chair has four casters to enhance its mobility. This transformation also provides freedom for users to combine them to form a socialable environment.

Brief

In collaboration with office systems company Teknion, this project explored new forms of workplace to address specific needs of the multi-generational workforce. Based on research and findings, the objective was to accommodate social behaviours in the workplace and to provide temporary working areas for collaborations and to manage the transition between static and flexible ways of working.

The project consisted of the following phases:
  • Research and report
  • Observations & Interviews
  • Competitive analysis
  • Concept Development
  • Model making and user testing

Research Findings

6 merging trends that explained the shifts occuring in workplaces:

Status does not define space

Workers are mobile

Workspaces are generally diverse

Space and time are flexible

Collaboration fosters creativity

Workstations are getting smaller

Observations

Observations and interviews from employees were summarized into 4 key issues that I wanted to address:

No available spots

Poor utilization of common area

Conference room is often occupied

Stationary furniture = static social environment

Four opportunity areas:

  • Shrinkage in space puts emphasis in the need to make efficient use of all available space
  • Satisfy the demand on break-out spaces for short-term group activities either in a temporary or permanent setting
  • Minimize the difficulty in furniture reconfiguration, essential elements for work: table, chair should be accessible
  • Modular furniture solutions can better facilitate individual and team work

Design Specifications

Design specifications outlined as measure of success:

  • It should adapt and encourage face-to-face interactions to provide a communicatory experience
  • It should be easily transportable and be optimized to utilize space
  • It should facilitate individual and collaborative work in a casual manner outside of the dedicated work area
  • Be able to combine together into a temporary space for group activity
